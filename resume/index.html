Kei Kreutler 🗝

is a writer, researcher, and strategist whose work explores how cultural narratives of technology shape what worlds we can build. I have an interdisciplinary background in arts, philosophy, and technology, with over 10 years of experience in organizational design.

Professional Experience

Inner Library, Consulting, 2024 to Present
My consulting work currently focuses on building a new cultural institution in the Bay Area, CA that will open to the public in 2027. I also advise on governance design, institutional memory, and long-term strategy, and I facilitate workshops that help organizations explore how they'll evolve over time. I write and ghostwrite essays, reports, and narrative slide decks on culture and technology.

Ethereum Foundation Summer of Protocols, Core Researcher, 2023
I researched and published work on memory in relation to protocols, specifically how ideas of memory from antiquity to the present changed with the advent of computing.

Gnosis, Director of Strategy and Communications, 2017 to 2022
I led strategy and communications at Gnosis, advising on company direction as the organization built decentralized software infrastructure for the Ethereum ecosystem. I framed product announcements both internally and externally, and I managed the communications team through launches for Safe (formerly Gnosis Safe), Gnosis Protocol, and GnosisDAO. I authored technical product communications as well as influential essays such as A Prehistory of DAOs, which became a widely cited text on decentralized governance. I also founded Gnosis Guild, a small team within the company that established an open standard for composable, modular software for organizations.
