Research

My research centers on philosophy of technology, and previous fellowship work explored the concept of memory in relation to “protocols,” specifically focused on how the concept of memory from antiquity to the present changed with the advent of computing.1 For the Summer of Protocols program, I now lead the Memory Research Group, a group that meets regularly to discuss a text related to the concept of memory across fields like philosophy, neuroscience, and computer science.

My upcoming book Artificial Memory on this research will be announced in more detail soon.

Some things about me

I grew up in the northeastern United States, and for a decade, I lived in Europe. From 2017 to 2022 in Berlin, Germany, I worked at Gnosis, building decentralized software infrastructure. While there, I co-created Gnosis Guild, a small team that established an open standard to support composable, modular software for organizations.2 In 2023, I participated as a Core Researcher in the Summer of Protocols program.3

I recently moved back to northeastern United States, where I live on a mountainside laying the groundwork for a long term project. I currently advise on organizational design, memory, and strategy through my consultancy Inner Library, contribute to Other Internet Research Institute, and sit on the Board of Regen Foundation.4, 5 Write to contact@keikreutler.net to get in touch.

When I’m away, you’ll often find me hiking Franconia Ridge or the Sangre de Cristo Mountains with family.
